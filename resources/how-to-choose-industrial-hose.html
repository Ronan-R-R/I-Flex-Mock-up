How to Choose the Right Industrial Hose

A guide for engineers and buyers on selecting hoses based on the STAMPED method.

Selecting the correct industrial hose is critical for safety, efficiency, and longevity. At L-Flex, we recommend using the STAMPED acronym to define your requirements before requesting a quote.

1. Size

You must know the Inside Diameter (ID) to match the flow requirements and the Outside Diameter (OD) if using external clamps or passing through tight spaces.

2. Temperature

Consider two temperatures: the temperature of the media being conveyed and the ambient temperature of the environment. Rubber compounds behave differently at extremes.

3. Application

What is the hose doing? Is it suction, discharge, or gravity flow? Will it be dragged on the ground (requiring a tough cover) or hung vertically?

4. Media

What is going through the hose? Be specific. "Oil" is not enough; is it crude oil, diesel, or hydraulic fluid? This determines the chemical compatibility of the tube liner.

5. Pressure

Know your Working Pressure (W.P.) and required Safety Factor. Surges and spikes in pressure should also be considered.

6. Ends

How will the hose be connected? Flanges, Camlocks, or threaded fittings? The style of end connection often dictates the assembly method.

7. Delivery

When do you need it? Custom hand-built hoses take time to manufacture, while standard stock can be shipped immediately.
