Hose Pressure Ratings Explained

Understanding Working Pressure, Burst Pressure, and Safety Factors.

Industrial hoses are rated by pressure to ensure the safety of operators. Misunderstanding these ratings is a common cause of failure.

Working Pressure (W.P.)

This is the maximum pressure at which the hose is designed to operate continuously. Your system pressure should never exceed this limit.

Burst Pressure

This is the pressure at which the hose will catastrophic fail (burst) during destructive testing. It is a theoretical limit, not an operating zone.

Safety Factor

The ratio between Burst Pressure and Working Pressure. For most industrial water hoses, a 3:1 ratio is standard. For critical applications like steam or concrete pumping, the ratio is often higher (e.g., 4:1 or higher) to account for pressure surges and wear.
